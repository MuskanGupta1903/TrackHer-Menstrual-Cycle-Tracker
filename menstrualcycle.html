What is the Menstrual Cycle?

The menstrual cycle is a natural, recurring process in the female reproductive system. It typically lasts between 21 and 35 days. If pregnancy does not occur, the uterine lining is shed through menstruation.

Phases of the Menstrual Cycle

There are four main phases in the menstrual cycle:

Menstrual Phase

The uterine lining is shed. This phase usually lasts 3–7 days.

Follicular Phase

Starts on day 1 and ends at ovulation. The body prepares to release an egg.

Ovulation

Occurs around day 14. The egg is released from the ovary.

Luteal Phase

From ovulation to menstruation. The body prepares for possible pregnancy.

Tips for a Healthy Menstrual Cycle

  • Track your cycle to understand your body’s rhythms.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in iron, folate, and omega-3s.
  • Stay active — light exercise reduces cramps and boosts mood.
  • Hydrate well and avoid too much caffeine or alcohol.
  • Consult your doctor for irregular or painful cycles.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

How long does a typical menstrual cycle last?

Between 21 and 35 days. Menstruation marks the beginning of the cycle.

What should I do if my cycle is irregular?

See a doctor to check for hormonal imbalances or other conditions.

Is it normal to have period pain?

Mild cramping is common. Severe pain should be evaluated by a professional.
