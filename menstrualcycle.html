What is the Menstrual Cycle?

The menstrual cycle is a natural, recurring process that happens in the female reproductive system. It typically lasts between 21 and 35 days. The cycle prepares the body for pregnancy, and if pregnancy does not occur, the lining of the uterus is shed, resulting in menstruation.

Phases of the Menstrual Cycle

There are four main phases in the menstrual cycle:

Menstrual Phase

This is the phase when the uterine lining is shed, leading to menstruation. It typically lasts 3 to 7 days.

Follicular Phase

It starts on the first day of menstruation and lasts until ovulation. The body prepares to release an egg.

Ovulation

Ovulation occurs when an egg is released from the ovary, typically around day 14 in a 28-day cycle.

Luteal Phase

This phase lasts from ovulation until the start of menstruation. The body prepares for a potential pregnancy.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

How long does a typical menstrual cycle last?

A typical cycle lasts between 21 and 35 days, with menstruation occurring at the start of the cycle.

What should I do if my cycle is irregular?

If your cycle is irregular, it's best to consult a healthcare provider to rule out any underlying conditions.

Is it normal to have period pain?

Some mild cramping is normal, but severe pain may indicate a medical issue like endometriosis. Seek medical advice if needed.
